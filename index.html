About the Novel

A boy no one noticed.
A man no one forgot.

Haruki Aizawa sits at Seat 38, last row, corner by the window. Teachers' eyes slide past him. Classmates form groups without him — not from cruelty, but from something more devastating: indifference.

But after school, two jeepneys away, he sits at a church piano and plays — and here, he is entirely different. Seen. Needed. Known.

This is the story of what happens when a boy decides to stop choosing smallness. Spanning high school through adulthood — faith, discipline, love, family, Saudi Arabia, and one piano that never stopped playing.

Haruki Aizawa

Protagonist

Seat 38. Church pianist. The invisible boy who becomes a visible man — not through recognition, but through discipline and faith.

Yuna Takahashi

Protagonist · RN

Front row. Third seat. She saw Haruki at the piano before school did — and stayed because of who he already was.
